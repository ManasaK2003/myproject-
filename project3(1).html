Everyone thought Namma Yatri will fail with its 0% commission for drivers, unlike Ola & Uber’s 40%  But to everyone’s SHOCK, Namma Yatri grabbed 25% of their market share in just 2 yrs! Auto rickshaws are a critical source of livelihood for over 3M drivers and an affordable mode of transportation for the masses. However, drivers’ earnings are often inconsistent when operating in a highly fragmented and poorly regulated industry.  Platforms like Ola & Uber charge a commission of 20-30% on the drivers’ earnings. Moreover, traditional passenger-centric models impose several restrictions on drivers, including penalties for rejecting rides or rigid fare structures.  Now to address these issues, Namma Yatri was built with driver-centric approach. Launched in 2022, it now operates in Bengaluru, Mysuru, Kochi, Kolkata, Asansol-Durgapur, Siliguri, Hyderabad, Chennai & Delhi. To fundamentally understand the drivers’ needs & pain points, they bought a few auto-rickshaws and started ferrying customers themselves. By driving autos & interacting with customers, they became part of the experience and faced the daily realities of both — drivers & passengers. They introduced a flexible pricing model addressing concerns raised by the drivers’ union, offering drivers two options: Rs.25 daily subscription for unlimited trips or Rs.3.5 per ride, with no charges after 10 trips. By charging a daily subscription fee of Rs.25-35, Namma Yatri earns just 1.66%-2.33% of a driver's daily income(assuming a driver earns Rs.1500/day), which is significantly lower than the 20% commission charged by traditional ride-hailing platforms. So, are these margins enough to cover the network’s cost? Well, this technologically sustainable startup is operating with Cost Minimization as one of its primary objectives. There are 4 key costs: Team, Maps, Servers and Marketing. To keep the expenses low, they kept their tech team lean by borrowing engineers from their parent co., Juspay. They also switched from Google Maps to Open Street Maps to reduce API costs. They’re efficiently scaling their business and effectively utilizing Public Digital Infrastructure to reduce their Mapping costs to Rs.2/ride, compared to other ride-hailing co.s who are typically spending Rs.12-15/ride. The co. believes that by developing a product correctly, and ensuring drivers are satisfied and feel a sense of belonging within your community, they won’t see you just as a rent-seeking platform. Now by going to the roots of the driver’s issue, they feel that the customer’s pain point of Lack of Transparency could be solved too. They're doing it by developing the app on the Beckn Protocol, an open-source system designed to make it easier for apps & services to connect. It works like a common language for apps, businesses & services, enabling them to share information without the need for a central controller to enhance transparency by providing full visibility into pricing, active rides, customer searches and drivers’ earnings. This approach eliminates hidden fees or commissions, and fosters trust.  Their commitment doesn’t stop here. Namma Yatri is defining the norms of urban commuting by engaging with the local drivers, building a strong local community, and tailoring services to meet the unique needs of each city. They launched the Yatri App in Kochi and the Yatri Sathi App in Kolkata with the Bengal Taxi Association for the city’s cab drivers. They have since transferred the management of these operations to the respective state govts. They have created goodwill among unions like Bengaluru’s Auto Rickshaw Drivers’ Union and regulators like the Bengal Govt but they have to form partnerships with Transit Operators such as metro and buses for last-mile connectivity.
